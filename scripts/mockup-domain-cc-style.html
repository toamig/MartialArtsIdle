Layout — fixed zones, temple at center

Cosmic backdrop: World Tree (sky), Void Gate (upper-left rift), Sky-Piercing Stele (right peak, light beam), Moonshrine (right peak silhouette)
Mid-distance hillsides: Outer Sect Hall + pilgrim path (left), Spirit Forge with smoke (right)
Temple anchor (center): your existing temple PNG drops in unchanged; cultivator & crystal visible through the arch
Foreground grounds: Spirit Herb Terraces (left of temple), Ancestral Pagoda (left-front), Training Ground with disciples (in front of temple), Beast Sanctuary pen (right of temple)

Why this is more ambitious-friendly than the temple-interior approach

  • Each producer has its own visual lane — a Spirit Forge can be a real building with smoke, not a brazier shoved in a corner.
  • The temple is the centerpiece, not the frame. Its existing PNG drops in unchanged. The wider domain extends AROUND it.
  • Adding new producers later (expansions, prestige-unlock tiers) means claiming a new zone in the domain — deeper sky, further mountain, new corner of grounds — without re-doing the temple.
  • Each producer's identity becomes architectural or natural: a hall, a forge, a pagoda, a sanctuary, a shrine, a stele, a tree. Easier to render. Easier to recognize. Easier to upgrade visually as count tiers escalate.

Art-cost ladder

Minimum (14 sprites + 1 wide BG)

One sprite per producer (10) + 4 "Mythic" variants where a sprite swap dramatically reads (tiger→dragon, sprout→tree, urn→crystal, pavilion-dim→lit). Plus one wide background PNG — a panoramic mountain-sky composition with a clear central area for the temple to sit on. ~2-3h of PixelLab work.

Polished (22 sprites + 1 BG)

Each producer gets a base + 1 visual tier escalation (Spirit Forge gets a "second smokestack", Beast Sanctuary gets a "bigger pen", etc.). Plus the cosmic-layer pieces (Dao Tree, Void Gate, Stele) need bespoke art. Half-day.

Wide-canvas tradeoff

You DO need a wider background painting for this. The temple PNG alone is too narrow for the new composition — you'd need a panoramic landscape with a temple-sized "hole" in the middle where the temple PNG drops in. OR: build the background out of CSS-generated layers (as this mockup does) and let it be procedural/atmospheric rather than painted. Loses some fidelity, gains independence from art.

Recommended path

Start with procedural CSS background (like this mockup) + 10 producer sprites. Ship. See if the procedural background feels right. If it does — great, no painting needed. If it doesn't — generate one panoramic BG PNG and swap it in. Decoupled from the producer art entirely.

Mobile / responsive note

The wider composition is naturally desktop-favoring. For mobile portrait, the layout would either compress (zones shrink, less ambient empty space) or pivot to a vertical scroll where the cosmic layer is at top, the temple is middle, and the grounds are bottom — player scrolls vertically to see their full domain. Both work; vertical scroll is more idle-game-conventional for mobile.
